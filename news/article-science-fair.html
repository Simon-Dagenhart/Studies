Maple Grove Takes First Place at the Regional Science Fair

June 12, 2026 · Written by the Maple Grove Communications Team

Two Maple Grove fifth-graders brought home top honors from this year's Tri-District Regional Science Fair, competing against more than 60 entries from schools across the region.

Amara Bell and Ben Whitfield spent nine weeks designing, building, and testing a multi-stage water-filtration model that used sand, gravel, activated charcoal, and cotton layers to clean visibly murky water. Their project, titled "Clean Water for Everyone: A Low-Cost Filtration Model," combined science content with a real-world engineering challenge.

"We wanted to build something that could actually help people, not just look cool on a poster," said Amara. "We tested five different filter combinations before we found one that worked."

The judging panel, made up of local engineers and university science faculty, praised the students' data collection and their ability to explain the engineering design process from start to finish.

A team effort

Their teacher, Mr. Kim, credited weekly after-school science club sessions for giving students the time and space to iterate on their designs. "These two rebuilt their filter four times," he said. "That kind of persistence is exactly what we hope every student takes away from a project like this."

The win adds to a strong year for Maple Grove's science program, which also saw three student teams advance to the district-level Invention Convention this spring.

Congratulations to Amara, Ben, and every Maple Grove student who competed this year!
